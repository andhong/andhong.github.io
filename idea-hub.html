| User Research | Experience Design | Branding | Education |

In 2014-2015 I was given the opportunity to create the MIT Museum's first making and tinkering space for the general public. I developed all of the educational activities, drawing from research being conducted at MIT and translating it easily digestible modules.

The goal of the customization interface was to create a novel design experience where non-technical users would be able to make alterations to the shape, color, and size of jewelry and then purchase their unique items. We achieved this by creating an interface that guided user choices towards an aesthically pleasing end design. Major decisions were also broken up into individual steps to break up the cognitive load of multiple complex decisions.
