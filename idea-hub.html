Idea Hub @MIT

| User Research | Experience Design | Branding | Education |

In 2014-2015 I was given the opportunity to create the MIT Museum's first making and tinkering space for the general public. I developed all of the educational activities, drawing from research being conducted at MIT and translating it easily digestible modules.

Activities ranged from digital design and 3D printing to paper circuits and coding with Scratch. I was in charge of facilitating activities, collaborating with MIT researchers and students to bring new content into the space, and conducting quantitative and qualitive analysis to evaluate the efficacy of the activities in the Idea Hub.
