Trove: Branding + Digital

Democratizing design by allowing anyone to customize 3D-printed jewelry - no experience required.

In 2015 I co-founded Trove alongside Brian Park and Tim Growney. Trove is a platform where users can discover, customize, and purchase their own unique 3D-printed jewelry pieces. My involvement was to cover the design of the brand, site, and UX from top to bottom along with continuous feature development.

The goal of the customization interface was to create a novel design experience where non-technical users would be able to make alterations to the shape, color, and size of jewelry and then purchase their unique items. We achieved this by creating an interface that guided user choices towards an aesthically pleasing end design. Major decisions were also broken up into individual steps to break up the cognitive load of multiple complex decisions.

The identity of the site is clean and minimal in order to allow the jewelry itself to be the center of attention. This also allows for a great deal of flexibility in allowing us to create personalized storefronts for influencers, affiliate marketers, and others who want to create their own brands within the Trove platform.
